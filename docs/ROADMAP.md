# Claygentforce Roadmap

The direction is now: **multi-perspective Salesforce consulting judgment simulation first, deterministic local War Room orchestration second, future AI-backed participant reasoning third**.

Scenario 001 remains the active proving ground. The Case fields, Flow, list view, permission set, and other Salesforce metadata are now grounding/reference artifacts, not the primary learner surface. The primary product experience is the **Salesforce Delivery War Room**.

The near-term product direction remains chat-first: scenario context should appear through stakeholder asks, participant messages, learner prompts, bounded choices, role-specific reactions, and professional assessments rather than large always-visible dashboards or metadata walkthroughs.

---

## Next priorities

1. **Add Security Reviewer playable perspective**  
   Extend Scenario 001 so the learner can experience the same engagement through access, sharing, field visibility, least-privilege, and manager-permission concerns.

2. **Add Business Analyst playable perspective**  
   Add a requirements/stakeholder lens focused on acceptance criteria, operational process, business language, user expectations, and success metrics.

3. **Add DevOps Engineer playable perspective**  
   Add a release-readiness lens focused on deployability, validation gates, rollback thinking, release windows, packaging confidence, and environment constraints.

4. **Deepen perspective-specific scoring models**  
   Continue making each playable perspective evaluate different professional judgment dimensions instead of reusing one generic score model.

5. **Add stronger dynamic disagreement patterns**  
   Increase realistic consulting tension when decisions improve one dimension while creating risk for another participant, such as QA confidence versus release timing or security rigor versus delivery pressure.

6. **Track participant confidence across a run**  
   Move beyond static per-decision sentiment by letting participant confidence shift over multiple decisions in a single War Room session.

7. **Add lightweight War Room timestamps and engagement cues**  
   Consider small, professional details such as relative timestamps, current stage labels, pressure indicators, or active-participant status only where they increase immersion without adding clutter.

8. **Refine professional assessment language**  
   Keep moving away from quiz/game language and toward consulting evaluation language such as delivery confidence, team consensus, stakeholder risk, release confidence, and unresolved evidence gaps.

9. **Prepare deterministic-to-AI replacement seams**  
   Keep local deterministic reaction maps clean enough that future Agentforce or other AI-backed participants can replace selected local response generation without changing the LWC rendering contract.

10. **Add adaptive participant follow-up questions**  
    Let participants challenge the learner with role-specific follow-up questions based on the selected perspective and prior decision path.

11. **Create Scenario 002 later**  
    Defer a second scenario until Scenario 001 proves the multi-perspective model across several roles. Good candidates include Agentforce hallucination risk, production deployment regression, Data Cloud identity resolution failure, or OmniStudio deployment regression.

12. **Build Scenario 002 from a War Room-first template**  
    When Scenario 002 begins, start from stakeholder ask, participant roster, professional perspectives, decision trees, reaction models, assessment dimensions, and optional metadata references. Do not assume every scenario needs new Salesforce metadata.

13. **Add saved session history later**  
    Track selected scenario, selected perspective, learner choices, participant reactions, assessment output, and reflection notes only after the local deterministic War Room flow is stable and useful.

14. **Build scenario authoring framework later**  
    Define reusable structures for scenario brief, participant roster, perspective definitions, decision options, reaction maps, assessment dimensions, and optional implementation artifacts.

15. **Create portfolio/demo narrative**  
    Shape the current War Room state into a concise story for Salesforce TA/FDE/Agentforce conversations: Claygentforce uses Salesforce as the first vertical for practicing consulting judgment under realistic delivery pressure.

16. **Record a short walkthrough**  
    Capture a concise demo showing role selection, Technical Architect perspective, QA Lead perspective, dynamic room reactions, War Room Assessment, and the same Scenario 001 engagement experienced through different professional lenses.

17. **Prepare AI integration narrative**  
    Explain how deterministic local participants can later become AI-backed role agents while preserving bounded choices, assessment consistency, and professional simulation design.

18. **Expand beyond Salesforce only after the core model is proven**  
    The long-term platform pattern could support other consulting domains, but Salesforce should remain the first vertical until the War Room model is strong and repeatable.
